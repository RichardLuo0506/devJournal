Frameworks

AngularJS
Despite my frustrations this is by far my best and is my go-to framework. Everything from project structure to coding habits I developed with this framework. I really hoped to like AngularJS 2.0 but it just isn't as attractive as other more nimble, less opinionated modern frameworks.
MeteorJS
This is the first framework I loved. I learned this during training camp so I really needed something both powerful and easy to learn. The isomorphic stack eliminated a lot of frustrations with inexplicable errors. Too bad there's very little demand for this skill.
Ruby on Rails
"My work done my way!" was my thinking when I was first exposed to "Convention over Configuration". I did not like Rails and all its conventions despite my liking Ruby. Since then I've gradually come around to this, especially when I started looking into Aurelia.


Responsive Design

Kurisutiiina!
You must pronounce this with an elongated "i" and with vibrato. As my designs got more complicated, I ditched Bootstrap and wrote my own SASS framework based on various frameworks I'd worked with in the past. My designs have gotten much simpler and minimalist as this allowed me to step away from grunt-work of polishing components and see the big picture.
Bootstrap
Oh bootstrap why must you be so opinionated? Tracking down CSS code overriden by the super-specific selectors and the "!important"s has been a nightmare. I still use this often for simpler things, and I've gotten good at being MORE opinionated than Bootstrap to do company work.
Foundation / Semantic UI / jQuery UI / Angular Material
Although I don't use these anymore, they've all contributed to how I design things.

Design

My Art History teacher always told me that whatever career I pursue, I mustn't squander my passion for art. So when I started seriously learning web programming over a year ago, I made a conscious effort to put more emphasis on the front-end.

Current

I like everything minimalist. I find that the simplest looking designs belie the thought behind them. For most of my work, I use Google's Material Design as a base and add new styles to my personal SASS framework.

Goal

For me, the next step of flat design is texturized flat + animations. I'm very much influnced by video games, and I hope to create experiences that are as intuitive and playful as those of well-made video games.

Future

For better or worse, many of our decisions are made based on emotion, I hope to create designs that can connect with the user emotionally.

Hobbies

Go

This board game was "discovered" 5,500 years ago. I say discovered because the concept is so simple and logical, that if aliens exist in the universe, they're playing it too. From this 19 x 19 board births more possible game positions than there are atoms in the known universe.
What I love about this game is that it's a competition of wisdom. Very often you must play moves that bring equal benefit to your opponent. You can figure out somebody's personality just from playing a game with him. Because life lessons can be derived from mathematical calculations on this board, I avoid learning the game and play by pure reading-ahead. This way Go becomes not just a hobby, but also a tool to measure my personal growth.
